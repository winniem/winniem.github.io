Customizable Keyboard

The customizable keyboard was a self-sponsored capstone project I worked on with two of my friends. Inspired by the difficulties multilingual people face when switching keyboard languages we decided to investigate whether it would be possible to design a keyboard whose individual keys had customizable displays. This would allow for the display of the key to update according to its output when the keyboard layout was changed.

In four months we designed and built a prototype with 12 functional keys and a GUI that allowed for a user to program the output of each key and save preferred keyboard layouts.

Snowbots

During university I had the opportunity to be a part of a student design team, UBC Snowbots, starting off as a general team member and eventually transitioning into a mechanical lead and co-captain. UBC Snowbots is a student design team that competed in a variety of autonomous robot competitions. The main competition we attended was the intelligent ground vehicle competition (IGVC) where the goal was to create a robot capable of navigating through an obstacle from one GPS waypoint to another autonomously. Our 2016 design report can be found here.

During my time on the team we also expanded to attend another competition, the Institute of Navigation's autonomous snowplow competition (ION ASC) , where we were required to reconfigure our robot to autonomously plow a field of snow. This required modifying our robot to handle low temperatures and installing stronger motors and a plow to plow snow.

Scanning Tunneling Microscope Upgrade

My second capstone project involved the upgrade of a scanning tunneling microscope (STM) for the UBC Laboratory of Atomic Imaging Research. The STM was initially designed as a faster lower resolution microscope to be used in open air for checking the quality of samples. Driven by a desire for higher quality scans we were tasked with designing and building a vacuum chamber and accompanying sample transfer mechanism. By the end of our four month project we were able to reach pressures of 2.5 x 10-5 mBar and had achieved a successful sample transfer using our custom transfer mechanism.
